# Sunday 2026-05-10 · Resilience

> "Tough times have not come to stay. They have come to pass."

Sunday is the day the week's accumulated weight catches up. Whatever happened Mon-Fri left a residue on you, and the temptation now is to read that residue as a verdict. It isn't. It's weather.

## The method

> "If you do what is easy, your life will be hard. If you do what is hard, your life will be easy."

Resilience isn't about being unbreakable. It's about choosing the harder thing on the day you most want the easier. Today the easy thing is to stay in bed and wait for Monday energy. The harder thing is to run while it's raining.

## The mindset

> "Adversity causes some people to break and others to break records."

The same hit that ends one person's week is the same hit that turns another person into the version they've been waiting to meet. The pressure isn't picking sides. The response is.

## Today's call

Pick one thing this Sunday that the broken version of you would skip. Maybe a long walk in the cold, or finishing the project you abandoned Wednesday. Do it for one reason: doing the thing teaches your nervous system who's actually in charge, regardless of mood.

Tough people last longer than tough times.
