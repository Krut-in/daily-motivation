# Tuesday 2026-06-30 · Focus

> "Every extra tab is a tiny promise to abandon the present."

Tuesday fragmentation can look like productivity. Tabs open, messages moving, notes half-written, a dozen threads alive. The real question is simpler: what did you move that mattered?

## The method

> "Focus means making the next hour inhospitable to distraction."

Design one hour with fewer exits. Close what is not needed, define the output, and make the cost of switching high enough that your attention stays where you placed it.

## The mindset

> "The work deepens when the exits close."

Your brain will call distraction relief because deep work creates friction. That does not make distraction restorative. It makes it seductive. Stay with the friction until it becomes progress.

## Today's call

Run one single-output hour. One task, one visible result, no parallel checking.

Depth begins when escape gets inconvenient.
