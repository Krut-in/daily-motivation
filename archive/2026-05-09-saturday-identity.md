# Saturday 2026-05-09 · Identity

> "Do something that sucks every single day of your life. That's how you grow."

Saturday is when most people lower the bar because no one's grading. The whole week was a performance for someone external. Today no one's watching, so today reveals who you actually are when there's no scoreboard.

## The method

> "The real grind is in the dark when nobody sees you."

Identity isn't built in front of cameras. It's built in the boring hour you don't post about, or the workout no one would have known you skipped. The Saturday rep no one applauds becomes the bar you cannot fall below on the worst Monday.

## The mindset

> "If no one thinks you're crazy, you're not yet operating to the outer limits of your potential."

The version of you that scares the comfortable people around you is usually the right one. If your work makes someone uncomfortable enough to call you 'too intense,' that's a signal. Don't soften it. Refine it.

## Today's call

Pick one thing today that you'd never tell anyone about. Maybe a workout you'd skip if no one would notice, or a piece of work you've been deferring with no real reason. Do it before the day softens into noise.

Identity is what survives the absence of an audience.
